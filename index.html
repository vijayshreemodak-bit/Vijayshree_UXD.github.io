01 — Overview

The current site informs — but never guides

The Manipur Tourism website had a fundamental mismatch between what users needed and what it offered. Travellers — especially first-timers — came looking for help planning a trip: itineraries, transport, safety, permits. What they found was a passive information archive, structured around government categories rather than human journeys.

My goal was to understand where and why the experience broke down, then redesign it around a single clear product strategy: help travellers plan and execute a memorable trip to Manipur in under 15 minutes.

02 — Research

Two lenses: what works elsewhere, what users actually need

Competitive Analysis
Four benchmarks, one clear gap
I analysed Kerala, Meghalaya, Rajasthan and Incredible India tourism websites — evaluated on navigation clarity, homepage engagement, trip planning support, trust signals, and mobile responsiveness.
  • Kerala, Meghalaya and Rajasthan all feature a prominent "Plan Your Trip" CTA on clean, visual homepages
  • Meghalaya's regional imagery creates authentic emotional connection — a direct parallel for Manipur
  • Incredible India excels at inspiring but lacks region-specific depth — making strong state websites essential
  • Manipur had no structured planning flow and buried trust-critical info like safety and permits
User Interviews — 5 Travellers
Not a discovery problem. A planning problem.
Most travellers discovered Manipur through word of mouth. The website's job wasn't inspiration — it was conversion: turning interest into a confident, booked trip. Three themes dominated every interview.
  • Planning felt overwhelming without a structured itinerary scaffold
  • Missing transport details and upfront costs created a "go/no-go" hesitation
  • Safety and accessibility info strongly affects decision-making — especially for first-timers
  • Mobile is the primary browsing device — the current site was broken on mobile
  • Static, text-heavy pages felt outdated and untrustworthy

Every insight mapped to a concrete solution

↓
Introduced a "Plan Your Trip" flow as the primary homepage CTA

Surfaced a visible Safety & Permits section early in the planning journey
Before Original structure
Prioritises information categories over user goals. Safety, permits and transport are invisible. No clear path from landing to planning.
After Redesigned structure
HOME
Plan Your Trip → Suggested / DIY Itinerary · Best Time to Visit · Where to Stay
Explore → Destinations · Cities · Heritage · Natural Wonders
Experiences → Festivals · Culture · Eco-Tourism · Adventure

Planning is the primary entry point. Trust-building surfaces early. Navigation depth reduced for mobile-first browsing. Structure mirrors how a traveller actually thinks.

08 — Learnings & Next Steps

What this project taught me

01
Simplicity is the design decision
The biggest insight from 4 weeks of work: too many steps or complications drive users away. Every screen I cut, every field I merged, and every label I simplified made the flow measurably better in testing. Restraint is a skill.
02
Trust is a UX problem, not just a content problem
Users didn't distrust Manipur — they distrusted the website. Surfacing safety, permits and updated content early in the journey is an architectural decision, not just a copywriting one. Where you put information signals how much you respect the user's anxiety.
03
Next steps: personalisation, transport depth, and regional authenticity
The path is correct — but there's a long way to go. Next iterations would deepen transport details with estimated costs, add authentic visual storytelling (local vlogs and blogs), and test the experience with international visitors who face a higher planning barrier.
